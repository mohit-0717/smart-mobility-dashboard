Grain & visualization analysis

Each visualization grain explained — dataset column → chart type → insight

Traffic flow & congestion
Columns: speed_kmh, congestion_index, hour_of_day
Dual-axis line chart
Speed and congestion are inversely correlated — as congestion rises above 0.7 during 8–10 AM and 5–7 PM, average speed drops to 28–32 km/h. AI signal timing can reduce peak congestion by ~15%.
2
EV charging sessions
Columns: session_type, session_count, station_id
Doughnut chart
Fast chargers account for 45% of all sessions despite being only 30% of stations, indicating significant unmet demand. Expanding fast charging by 40% in South/East zones is recommended.
3
Accident frequency trend
Columns: accident_count, month, year
Grouped bar chart
2024 shows an 18% reduction in accidents vs 2023. Monsoon months (Jul–Sep) remain high-risk. Predictive AI flagging reduced rear-end collisions by 22% in North Zone.
4
Accident cause distribution
Columns: cause_category, cause_count
Polar area chart
Speeding (35%) and weather (22%) together account for 57% of all incidents. Targeted speed cameras at 12 identified hotspot junctions could reduce incidents by a projected 22%.
5
Bus on-time performance
Columns: route_id, on_time_pct, target_pct
Horizontal progress bars
Routes 12 (79%) and 23 (72%) fall below the 85% target. Both routes share a common bottleneck at Junction 7. Signal priority systems could recover 8–12% on-time improvement.
6
Transport mode share
Columns: transport_mode, trip_count
Icon-based proportion cards
Private cars dominate at 45%. Shifting just 10% of car trips to bus/metro would reduce congestion index citywide by 0.12 points and lower carbon emissions by an estimated 8,400 tonnes/year.
7
Weekly zone volume
Columns: zone, day_of_week, vehicle_count
Multi-line area chart
North Zone consistently peaks on Fridays (14,100 vehicles/hr), 23% above the weekly average. Weekend volumes drop by 25% across all zones, suggesting flexible infrastructure deployment.
8
City congestion heatmap
Columns: grid_cell_id, congestion_index, zone
Grid heatmap
The 10×10 heatmap reveals spatial clustering of high congestion in the North-Central corridor. 12% of grid cells account for 68% of total delay time, pinpointing priority intervention zones.

Claude AI — Dashboard Analysis
Traffic Flow: North Zone shows 15% higher congestion during peak hours (8–10 AM, 5–7 PM). AI-based dynamic signal timing can reduce peak congestion index from 0.81 to below 0.60, saving an estimated 22 minutes of average commute time.
EV Infrastructure: Fast chargers handle 45% of sessions from only 30% of stations. Recommend a 40% capacity expansion in South and East zones with demand-prediction algorithms to pre-allocate charging slots.
Accident Prediction: 2024 shows an 18% reduction vs 2023. Speeding (35%) remains top cause. Deploying AI-powered speed enforcement at 12 identified hotspot junctions projects a further 22% reduction.
Weather Impact: Rainy conditions increase accident rate by +34% and reduce average speed by 18 km/h. Foggy conditions increase accident risk by +48%. Adaptive speed limits triggered by weather sensors are recommended.
Public Transport: Routes 12 and 23 underperform below 85% target. A traffic signal priority system and dedicated bus lanes at Junction 7 could recover 8–12% on-time performance within one quarter.
Overall Recommendation: Implement citywide IoT-based adaptive traffic management. Integrating EV demand forecasting, real-time public transport rerouting, and AI accident prediction projects a 20% overall efficiency improvement by Q3 2025.
